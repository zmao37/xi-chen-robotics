Xi Chen

Researcher in Robotics and Embodied AI

pcchenxi@gmail.com | Google Scholar | Phone: +86 135 1081 0137

My research focuses on building intelligent robotic systems that can generalize across embodiments, tasks, and real-world environments.

To this end, I work at the intersection of embodied AI, robot learning, and multi-robot systems, developing data-driven methods for cross-robot adaptation, robust policy learning from heterogeneous data, and coordination in heterogeneous multi-robot systems.

My long-term goal is to enable transferable and deployable robot intelligence that can learn from collective experience, share knowledge across heterogeneous robots, and operate reliably in complex real-world settings.

Selected Research Projects

Intelligent Airport Security Inspection System 2024 – 2026
Project Lead, BIGAI
  • Leading the development of a distributed heterogeneous multi-robot system for intelligent airport security inspection, with the goal of improving operational efficiency and reducing safety risks in complex airport environments.
  • Directing system design and integration, including multi-robot coordination, dynamic task allocation, and multimodal human–robot interaction, toward deployment-oriented evaluation in real-world settings.
Intention-Aligned Imitation Learning for Heterogeneous Robots 2021 – 2024
Lead Researcher, Tsinghua University
  • Developed a cross-robot imitation learning framework that transfers behaviors through shared high-level intention representations rather than low-level action matching.
  • Led framework design and large-scale experimental validation across seven robot platforms and 30 collaborative scenarios, resulting in a publication in Science Robotics.
CENTAURO: Mobile Manipulation Robot for Disaster Response 2018 – 2020
Research Contributor, KTH
  • Developed learning-based terrain classification and adaptive locomotion methods for a wheel-legged disaster-response robot operating on complex and unstructured surfaces.
  • Contributed to system integration and real-world evaluation of learning-based mobility modules within the CENTAURO platform.
